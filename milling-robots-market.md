<p><p>Milling robots are categorized by their degrees of freedom, which affects their flexibility and precision in machining tasks. </p><p>- **4-axis robots** typically handle simple milling operations, offering movement along three linear axes and rotation around one axis. </p><p>- **5-axis robots** provide enhanced agility, allowing for complex geometries and contours through additional rotational movement.</p><p>- **6-axis robots** deliver superior versatility and precision, accommodating intricate designs with full spatial control.</p><p>- **7-axis robots** further extend capabilities, enabling advanced positional adjustments for even more complex machining applications. </p><p>- **Other market** refers to specialized configurations or hybrid systems that integrate features from various types.</p></p>
