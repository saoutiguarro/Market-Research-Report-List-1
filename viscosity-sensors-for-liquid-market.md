<p><strong>The Viscosity Sensors for Liquid Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Sonic Sensor</li><li>Mechanical Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Viscosity sensors for the liquid market primarily include sonic and mechanical sensors. Sonic sensors utilize sound waves to assess liquid viscosity by measuring the speed of wave propagation through the fluid. In contrast, mechanical sensors use moving elements, such as rotating spindles or oscillating plates, to determine viscosity based on resistance encountered in the liquid. Both types offer distinct advantages, with sonic sensors typically providing non-invasive measurements, while mechanical sensors are often favored for direct and extensive interactions with the fluid being tested.</p></p>
