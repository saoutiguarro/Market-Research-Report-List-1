<p><p>Oil storage terminals can be categorized into two main markets: strategic reserves and commercial reserves. Strategic reserves are maintained by governments or agencies to ensure national energy security during crises, such as supply disruptions or geopolitical tensions. In contrast, commercial reserves are operated by private companies to store excess oil for trading, speculation, or to manage supply and demand. Both markets play crucial roles in stabilizing oil prices and ensuring consistent energy availability in various scenarios.</p></p>
