<p><p>The preclinical X-ray imaging market is divided into two main types: structural imaging and functional imaging. Structural imaging focuses on the physical architecture of tissues and organs, enabling researchers to visualize anatomical details and detect morphological changes. In contrast, functional imaging assesses physiological processes, such as blood flow or metabolic activity, providing insights into biological function. Together, these imaging types facilitate comprehensive analysis in preclinical research, enhancing drug development and understanding of disease mechanisms.</p></p>
