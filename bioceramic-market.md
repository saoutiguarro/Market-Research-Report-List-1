<p><p>The bioceramic market encompasses various types, primarily categorized into alumina-type and zirconia-type bioceramics, along with other specialty ceramics. Alumina-type bioceramics are known for their excellent mechanical strength and biocompatibility, making them ideal for load-bearing applications like dental and orthopedic implants. Zirconia-type bioceramics offer superior toughness and aesthetic appeal, often used in dental crowns and prosthetics. Other bioceramics include bioactive glasses and calcium phosphates, which promote bone regeneration and healing, expanding their use in regenerative medicine and tissue engineering.</p></p>
